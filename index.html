About

01

I am a Research Associate at the AI Healthcare Laboratory, Carnegie Mellon University Africa, where my work sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence and healthcare, with a focus on computer vision for medical imaging.

I hold a Master's degree in Information Technology with a concentration in data science and applied machine learning, and a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from St. Joseph University, Tanzania. This trajectory, from foundational engineering through applied ML, has shaped my interest in building AI systems that translate technical advances into clinical impact.

I am currently working on the application of transformer models for parasite detection in blood smear images, exploring how modern deep learning architectures can support faster, more accessible diagnostics. More broadly, I am passionate about leveraging technology to advance healthcare, particularly in low-resource clinical settings where reliable diagnostic tools can make the greatest difference.

MalariaVision

A deep learning pipeline for automated classification of blood smear cells as parasitized or uninfected. Six architectures evaluated on the NIH Malaria Cell Image dataset to identify the most reliable approach for low-resource diagnostic deployment.
